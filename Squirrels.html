Squirrels

Squirrels are active, agile creatures often found in trees and wooded areas, but they can become a problem when they invade homes and properties. They are known to chew through wires, insulation, and wood, causing significant damage and potential fire hazards.

How They Invade Homes

  • Squirrels can enter homes through small gaps, cracks, or openings in roofs, soffits, and eaves.
  • They may chew through vents, attic screens, and even wooden structures to gain access.
  • Squirrels can use overhanging tree branches to reach rooftops and explore potential entry points.
  • They often nest in attics, chimneys, and wall voids, where they create nests out of insulation and other materials.
  • Open garage doors, windows, or crawl spaces can also be entry points for squirrels.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Trim tree branches that are close to your roof to prevent easy access to the house.
  • Seal gaps, holes, and cracks in your roof, eaves, and foundation to block entry points.
  • Use chimney caps and attic screens to keep squirrels from entering through vents or chimneys.
  • Regularly inspect and maintain attic and roof areas to identify and repair any damage caused by squirrels
  • Remove food sources like birdseed or fallen fruits from your yard, which may attract squirrels.
  • Install squirrel-proof barriers or traps in areas where they are likely to enter or cause damage.
