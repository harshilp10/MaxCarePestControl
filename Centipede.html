Centipedes

House centipedes often enter homes in search of food, moisture and shelter. They find thier way into homes through various mean, such as cracks/gaps, basement/crawl space, drain/pipes, openings around utility lines, doorways, and windows.

How They Invade Homes

  • They can enter through small cracks, gaps, and crevices in walls, foundations, and around doors and windows.
  • These areas provide the dark, damp conditions that centipedes prefer.
  • Centipedes can come through drains, pipes, and other plumbing fixtures.
  • Gaps around utility lines, vents, and cables can serve as entry points.
  • They can slip through under doorways and around poorly sealed windows.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Close off any cracks, gaps, and crevices in walls, foundations, and around doors and windows.
  • Fix any leaks, use dehumidifiers, and ensure proper ventilation in basements, crawl spaces, and bathrooms.
  • Keep living areas clean and free of clutter, which can provide hiding spots for centipedes.
  • Clear away leaves, grass clippings, and other organic debris from around the exterior of your home.
  • Keep the lawn mowed, bushes trimmed, and remove any piles of wood or debris that could serve as shelter.
  • Set up traps and use insecticides in areas where centipedes are commonly found.
