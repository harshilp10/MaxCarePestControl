Bed Bugs

Bed bugs are small, parasitic insects that primarily feed on the blood of humans and animals. They find thier way into homes through various mean, such as traveling on luggage and clothing, used furniture and bedding, public places, shared housing, and visitors.

How They Invade Homes

  • Bed bugs often hitch a ride on luggage, clothing, and personal belongings when people travel.
  • Bringing second-hand furniture, mattresses, or bedding into the home can introduce bed bugs.
  • Bed bugs can be picked up in public places such as hotels, public transportation, theaters, and even offices.
  • In multi-unit buildings, bed bugs can move between apartments through walls, electrical outlets, and shared laundry facilities.
  • Guests visiting your home can inadvertently bring bed bugs with them.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Carefully inspect and clean second-hand furniture, mattresses, and bedding before bringing them into your home.
  • Encase mattresses and box springs in protective covers designed to keep bed bugs out.
  • Keep your home clutter-free to reduce hiding places for bed bugs.
  • Vacuum floors, carpets, and furniture regularly to remove bed bugs and their eggs.
  • Inspect hotel rooms for signs of bed bugs, keep luggage off the floor, and wash and dry clothing on high heat after returning home.
  • Seal any cracks and crevices in walls, baseboards, and furniture to eliminate hiding spots.
  • Use bed bug interceptors on bed legs to monitor for the presence of bed bugs.
