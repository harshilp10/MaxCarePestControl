Wasps

Wasps are aggressive insects that can become a serious problem when they build nests near homes. They often seek out sheltered areas for nesting and can be a danger to humans, especially for those allergic to their stings.

How They Invade Homes

  • Wasps often build nests in sheltered areas such as eaves, attics, wall cavities, or under porches.
  • They can enter homes through open windows, doors, or vents that are not properly screened.
  • Wasps may use cracks and gaps around foundations, walls, and roofs to enter interior spaces like attics or basements.
  • They are attracted to sugary foods and drinks, making outdoor dining areas and kitchens a target for wasps seeking food.
  • Nests may also be built inside hollow spaces, such as tree trunks, sheds, garages, or even outdoor furniture.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Regularly inspect your home and outdoor areas for the early signs of wasp nests, especially in eaves, sheds, and attics.
  • Seal any cracks, gaps, or holes around windows, doors, and roofs to prevent wasps from entering or building nests inside.
  • Keep food and sugary drinks covered when outdoors, and promptly clean up spills and food scraps to avoid attracting wasps.
  • Use screens on windows, doors, and vents to block wasps from entering the home.
  • Trim trees, shrubs, and vegetation near the house to eliminate potential nesting sites.
  • If you find a wasp nest, avoid disturbing it and contact a pest control professional for safe removal.
