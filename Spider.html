Spiders

Spiders are common pests that can find their way into homes through various entry points. They can be both beneficial, by controlling other insect populations, and a nuisance when they invade living spaces.

How They Invade Homes

  • Spiders often enter homes through cracks and gaps around windows, doors, and foundations.
  • They can crawl through vents, chimneys, and gaps in utility lines to access the interior of a house.
  • Spiders can hitch a ride on items brought into the home, such as boxes, firewood, or plants.
  • Open doors and windows or poorly sealed areas can provide easy entry points.
  • Spiders may also come in through unsealed or improperly maintained exterior openings, such as cracks in walls or gaps around eaves.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Seal all cracks and gaps around windows, doors, and foundations to prevent spiders from entering.
  • Regularly clean and vacuum your home, especially corners and under furniture, to remove spider webs and egg sacs.
  • Reduce clutter around your home to minimize potential hiding spots for spiders.
  • Keep outdoor lights off or use yellow bug lights to reduce the number of insects that may attract spiders.
  • Trim back shrubs, trees, and vegetation away from the house to minimize potential pathways for spiders to reach the home.
  • Use spider repellents or insecticides in areas where spiders are commonly seen, such as corners and around windows and doors.
