Rats and Mice

Rats and Mice are highly skilled at finding their ways into homes through various means, such as cracks and holes, pipes, vents and chimneys, roof, basement and garages.

How They Invade Homes

  • They can squeeze through small cracks, holes, and gaps in walls, foundations, and around windows and doors.
  • They often enter through openings around utility pipes and wiring.
  • These rodents can use vents, chimneys, and other openings to access the interior of a house.
  • They can climb trees, walls, and utility lines to access roofs and enter through gaps in eaves or roof tiles.
  • Open doors, windows, or poorly sealed areas in basements and garages provide easy entry points.

How to Prevent Infestations

  • Close off any cracks, gaps, and holes around the foundation, windows, doors, and utility pipes.
  • Keep living areas clean and free of food debris. Store food in airtight containers and clean up spills immediately.
  • Use sealed trash cans and dispose of garbage regularly.
  • Remove clutter, both inside and outside the home, to reduce potential nesting sites.
  • Keep trees, shrubs, and vegetation trimmed and away from the house to reduce roof access.
  • Set up traps and baits in areas where rodents are likely to travel, such as along walls and near entry points.
